A Timeline of Franklin Delano Rooselvelt's Life and Accomplishments

  • 1882 - Born in Hyde Park, New York
  • 1903 - Graduates from Harvard University with a Bachelor of Arts in History
  • 1905 - Marries the love of his life Eleanor Roosevelt against the overprotective will of his mother, Sara Delano
  • 1911 - Taking his father's political affiliation, FDR Became a Democratic New York State Senator and was a leader against the political machine of Tammany Hall
  • 1913 - With Roosevelt's support of Wilson's presidential campaign in 1912, Franklin was appointed the Assistant Secretary of the Navy
  • 1929 - Elected as the Governor of New York, setting FDR up as a contender for the next presidential election
  • 1932 - Became elected as the 32nd President of the United States of America
  • 1933 - Created his First New Deal to lead the United States out of the Great Depression
  • 1934 - Created the Second New Deal which established Social Security and the Works Progress Administration
  • 1936 - FDR wins a landslide election winning 60.8% of the vote and losing only states of Maine and Vermont
  • 1940 - FDR passes the Selective training and Service Act of 1940 increasing the Army size from 189,000 in 1939 to 1.4 million by mid 1941
  • 1940 - Defies the 2-term President tradition and runs and wins a 3rd term election
  • 1941 - After the Bombing of Pearl Harbor, Rooselevet signs a declaration of War against Japan
  • 1944 - Easily secured a fourth term by campaigning in favor of the United Nations and strong International community
  • 1945 - FDR dies less than 3 months into his fourth term and dies at the age of 63
